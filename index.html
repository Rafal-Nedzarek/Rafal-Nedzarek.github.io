About Me

2012 – finished my higher education (MA in English Studies, MA in International Relations and Security) with flying colors and began work as freelance translator.

2014 – took a few Python courses on Coursera (starting with Python for Everybody) and was able to use the knowledge to automate simple tasks in my new office job as Language Lead. There, I gained my first experience with Jira, using it to coordinate linguistic QA.

2017 – worked as Technical Specialist at a translation company. I administered the XTRF system and served as 1st line internal support for stakeholders at various levels of technical knowledge. While handling various file formats in the MemoQ CAT tool, I gained basic XML and HTML knowledge. I followed that up with basic CSS and JavaScript training at FreeCodeCamp to better understand how front-end works.

2018 – got hired as Software Support Engineer at a fintech company. I expanded my SQL knowledge and aquired some Oracle DB troubleshooting skills. Additionally, I gained first-hand experience of working as part of an agile development team while supporting business clients via e-mail, chat and Jira. I also passed the ISTQB Foundation Level exam.

2019 – after relocating to Dublin, Ireland I am looking for an IT job that would utilize and develop my hard-earned skills.

My Projects

My biggest struggle over the years has been to keep my programming a sustained effort. Having to juggle full-time work at the office with steady freelance translation contracts, I often had to postpone my learning to a later date.

I decided I would need to work on personal projects – not only to showcase my skills but to keep my focus. To finally connect various technologies I had learnt, I chose the Django framework. I started with the Python and Django Full Stack Web Developer Bootcamp course on Udemy and quickly supplemented it with official Django documentation, Stack Overflow and other sources.

After several weeks of learning and experimenting, I have deployed my first Django app to Heroku. Visitors can register, login, and perform CRUD operations on tables stored in a Heroku Postgres database. The app’s code can be found on my GitHub profile page.
